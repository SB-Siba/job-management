BEDSIDE PATIENT ATTENDANT

A bedside patient attendant provides essential care and support to patients in healthcare settings or at home. They assist with daily activities such as bathing, dressing, feeding, and mobility. Attendants monitor vital signs, administer medication, and ensure patients' comfort and safety. They also offer emotional support, engaging patients in conversation and addressing their concerns. By maintaining a clean and organized environment, they help prevent infections and promote recovery. Bedside attendants work under the supervision of medical professionals, following care plans and reporting changes in patients' conditions to ensure timely medical intervention. Their compassionate care is vital for patient well-being.

SECURITY

Security is the state of being protected against threats or danger. It encompasses measures and practices designed to safeguard individuals, organizations, and assets from various risks, such as physical harm, theft, and espionage. Effective security involves implementing policies, procedures, and technologies to prevent, detect, and respond to potential threats. It includes physical security measures like locks and surveillance, cybersecurity strategies such as firewalls and encryption, and procedural safeguards like access controls and employee training. Overall, security aims to ensure safety, confidentiality, integrity, and availability of resources and information.
